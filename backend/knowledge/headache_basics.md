# Headache – General Information (Non-Diagnostic)

## Common, Non-Urgent Causes
- Tension-type headaches: dull, tight, band-like pressure around the head.
- Dehydration: headache with dry mouth, fatigue, darker urine.
- Poor sleep or stress: headaches after long screen time or lack of rest.
- Caffeine changes: too much or sudden withdrawal.

## Basic Self-Care Steps
- Drink water regularly over the next few hours.
- Rest in a quiet, dark room.
- Use gentle neck and shoulder stretches.
- Over-the-counter pain relief may help if normally safe for you.

## Red Flags (Seek Urgent Care)
- Sudden, severe headache (“worst headache of life”).
- Headache with weakness, numbness, confusion, or trouble speaking.
- Headache after a head injury.
- Fever with stiff neck or rash.
- New headache that keeps getting worse.

## Notes for Triage Systems
- Sudden onset + severe intensity is higher risk.
- Neurologic symptoms override other factors.
- Gradual onset with mild–moderate severity is often lower risk.
